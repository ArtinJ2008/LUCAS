- Correlations: pressure, water/rock ratio, carbon inventory, alteration extent

## Definition

Temperature of the upstream water-rock reaction zone that produces the outgoing
fluid composition. It is not the temperature of the downstream prebiotic
reaction domain.

## Applicability

- Pressure: selected experiments at 50 MPa
- Starting fluid: CO$_2$-rich experimental composition
- Rock: synthetic komatiite
- Time/scale: batch hydrothermal experiment; natural through-flow unresolved

## Extraction and transformation

Converted degrees Celsius to kelvin. The 573.15–623.15 K bracket targets the
high-temperature behavior discussed across the paper and connected experiments;
it is not a measured Hadean distribution.

## Uncertainty

Rock composition, CO$_2$, water/rock ratio, residence time, permeability, and
reaction progress may matter as much as temperature. A one-dimensional
temperature prior would be misleading.

## Validation and cross-checks

First reproduce the discrete experiment outputs at their exact conditions. Then
test interpolation and held-out temperatures before using a continuous model.

## Sensitivity and impact

Temperature changes alteration, carbonate stability, H$_2$ generation,
alkalinity, phase stability, and heat delivered downstream.

## Conflicting evidence

Low-temperature modern Lost-City-like models are a competing source
architecture. They cannot be combined with high-temperature komatiite output
without an explicit geological path.
